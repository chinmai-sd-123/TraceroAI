Data Residency

Available Regions
-----------------
Workspaces can be created in a US region or an EU region. The region you choose
determines where your primary data is stored and processed. EU-region workspaces keep
customer data within EU data centers to support GDPR data-residency requirements.

Changing Regions
----------------
A workspace region cannot be changed after creation. This is a hard constraint: region
is fixed at creation time because data is physically provisioned in that region. To move
to a different region you must contact support to arrange a migration, which copies your
data to a new workspace in the target region and then decommissions the old one.

Migration Process
-----------------
A region migration is scheduled with support and typically runs during a maintenance
window. During the migration the source workspace is placed in read-only mode to ensure
consistency. Downtime depends on data volume but is usually under an hour for typical
accounts. After verification, traffic is cut over to the new region and the old
workspace's data is securely deleted within 35 days.

Latency Considerations
----------------------
Choose the region closest to your users. Cross-region access still works but adds
network latency; for latency-sensitive workloads, keeping the workspace and your
application in the same region is recommended.
